Working in a World Post Covid

Covid and the NYC Workforce

By: Sade Nabahe


Two crises: Health & The Economy


In the past few weeks, New York City has been one of the epicenters for the coronavirus.


According to the most recent data from April 9th, there are 81,803 reported cases and 4,571 deaths in the NYC alone.


Americans inside and most businesses to temporarily shut their doors in order to contain the spread of the virus.


However, this is causing another crisis in NYC and the United States. An economic crisis.



Living in Limbo

In the past three weeks, 16.6 million Americans lost their jobs.


Filed weekly unemployment claims around the country continue to break records - 6.6 million claims for the past two weeks.



This is 10x more than were filed during the height of the Great Recession in 2008.



Unfortunately, this is only predicted to increase in the following weeks.


Studies by Goldman Sachs and J.P. Morgan estimated job losses between 8.5 to 14 million workers by summer, but the United States has clearly passed this number in a very short window.










Industry Specific Impacts

Some industries were hurt more than others. Hospitality, food services and retail trade experienced the most dramatic cuts to employment.



But other groups of workers such as healthcare workers, cashiers and janitors are facing different challenges. Lots of work, but little pay and protections.



Cashiers are by far worst off in terms of wage levels and level of contact to the virus.



With a yearly salary of $22,000 without health insurance or additional benefits put low-income populations more at risk of contracting the virus.



Healthcare assistants and nurses are close behind, making only $24,000 per year and even closer to the frontlines.



That being said there is much work to be done in order to protect the most vulnerable. For those without jobs, health coverage and increased unemployment benefits are a start. And for those working but putting themselves and families at risk, doubling wages among healthcare benefits are absolutely necessary.
